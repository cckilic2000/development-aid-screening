## Executive Summary

The ILO is calling for non-profit implementing partners to deliver accelerated Competency-Based Training (CBT) and structured Work-Based Learning (WBL) for approximately 1,100 displaced and vulnerable beneficiaries in the Aleppo-Idleb and Hamah governorates of Syria. The assignment — funded under the PROSPECTS Partnership Phase II Opportunity Fund (UNHCR, UNICEF, ILO) — requires labour market validation, competency standards development, trainer and assessor capacity building, CBT delivery for ~300 participants, and WBL facilitation for ~1,100 participants across reconstruction-linked sectors including manufacturing, renewable energy, agrifood, textiles, and ICT. A contractual outcome of at least 50% employment or self-employment transition within three months of WBL completion is required. The submission deadline is 7 July 2026, and queries must be submitted by 30 June 2026.

## Detailed Assignment Summary

The assignment responds to Syria's post-conflict recovery context following "years of conflict and economic decline" that have "severely weakened Syria's infrastructure, public services, labour market systems, and productive sectors." The December 2024 political transition and the lifting of international sanctions have created new conditions for economic reintegration programming.

The implementing partner is expected to carry out four main functions. First, validate existing labour market and skills anticipation studies covering reconstruction manufacturing, textile and garment, renewable energy, agrifood processing, greenhouse-related occupations, food processing, and selected ICT and digital occupations. Second, create or update competency standards, training materials, and assessments, and train both institutional trainers and in-company trainers and assessors. Third, deliver accelerated (~3-month) modular CBT programmes for approximately 300 beneficiaries requiring structured competency development prior to workplace placement, and structured remunerated WBL for approximately 1,100 beneficiaries. Fourth, implement competency-based assessments, organise certification in partnership with national institutions or private sector bodies, and support a minimum of 50% of WBL completers into wage or self-employment within three months of graduation, tracked through ILO Service Tracker.

Target beneficiaries are returnees, internally displaced persons, vulnerable youth, women (minimum 30%), and persons with disabilities (minimum 5%). Beneficiaries are to be prioritised from those referred through ILO Public Employment Services. The assignment aligns with ILO Conventions No. 142, Recommendation No. 195, Recommendation No. 205, Recommendation No. 208, the EU Durable Solutions Programme, the ILO Programme of Support for Syria, and the United Nations Transitional Action Plan for Syria.

## Key Deliverables

- Labour market validation study covering priority reconstruction sectors and occupations
- Updated or newly created competency standards and training materials
- Trained trainers and assessors, including in-company trainers, with OSH and Fundamental Principles and Rights at Work mainstreamed
- CBT programmes delivered to approximately 300 beneficiaries (accelerated, ~3-month modular format)
- Structured remunerated WBL placements for approximately 1,100 beneficiaries (including ≥30% women, ≥5% persons with disabilities)
- Formative and summative competency-based assessments conducted
- Certifications issued in partnership with national institutions or private sector bodies
- Minimum 50% of WBL completers transitioned to wage employment or self-employment within 3 months of graduation
- Employment outcomes tracked through ILO Service Tracker

## Capacity Building Components

- **Training of Trainers (ToT):** Train institutional trainers and in-company trainers on updated competency standards and assessment tools
- **Assessor development:** Train assessors on competency-based assessment methods
- **Curriculum development:** Create or update training materials, competency standards, and formative/summative assessment instruments
- **OSH and rights mainstreaming:** Integrate occupational safety and health, Fundamental Principles and Rights at Work, and awareness of the national labour code into all training content
- **National certification systems:** Partner with national institutions and private sector bodies to organise and recognise competency certification
- **Employment transition support:** Coaching/support for minimum 50% of WBL graduates into employment or self-employment

## Risks / Concerns

- **Non-profit eligibility only:** The notice explicitly limits applicants to "non-profit organizations and entities" — for-profit consulting firms are ineligible without a non-profit implementing partner arrangement.
- **Budget undisclosed:** Contract value is not published; the financial scope of covering ~1,100 beneficiaries across CBT and WBL cannot be assessed from the public notice.
- **Reference number missing:** No reference number is stated in the public notice, complicating procurement tracking.
- **Conflict-affected location:** Operational activities in Aleppo-Idleb and Hamah governorates carry security, logistics, and access risks inherent to post-conflict and transitional contexts.
- **Tight query window:** Technical queries must be submitted by 30 June 2026 — only 13 days from publication — limiting time for due diligence before submission.
- **Performance obligation:** A minimum 50% employment transition outcome within 3 months is a contractual deliverable, creating financial and reputational risk if labour market conditions deteriorate.
- **Full ToR not on public page:** Detailed scope, deliverables, team requirements, and evaluation criteria are in the Terms of Reference attached to the UNGM notice and not reproduced in the public summary.
