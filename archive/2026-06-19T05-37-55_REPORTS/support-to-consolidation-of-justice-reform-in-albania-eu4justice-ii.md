## Executive Summary

EU4Justice II is a EUR 13 million, 48-month technical assistance contract procured by the European Commission under IPA III to consolidate Albania's Justice Reform 2016 across four pillars: judicial efficiency; judicial independence and accountability; quality of justice and legal education; and transparency and access to justice. The project targets Courts, Prosecution Offices, legal training institutions (including the Faculty of Law), governance bodies of the judiciary, the Ministry of Justice and its subordinate bodies, and the legal professions. It is a direct continuation of EU4Justice I (2023–2026) and is tied to Albania's Chapter 23 EU accession obligations and Interim and closing benchmarks. The first-stage deadline for receipt of requests to participate is 14 August 2026; shortlisting will result in 4 to 8 candidates invited to the second stage. Award weighting is 80% quality and 20% price.

## Detailed Assignment Summary

EU4Justice II is framed as the continuation of EU4Justice I (2023–2026) under IPA 2025, building on practices already developed in the first phase. The stated objective is to consolidate the implementation of the Justice Reform 2016 by ensuring technical expertise to the judiciary and its allied institutions.

The project is organised around four integrated pillars. Pillar 1 (judicial efficiency) targets operational and procedural improvements across Courts and Prosecution Offices. Pillar 2 (judicial independence and accountability) addresses governance and oversight mechanisms for the judiciary. Pillar 3 (quality of justice and legal education) engages legal training institutions, including the Faculty of Law, to raise professional standards within the legal community. Pillar 4 (transparency and access to justice) extends the reform's reach to legal aid, bailiffs, the National Chamber of Advocates, and the Chamber of Mediation.

The implementing period is 2027–2031 (48 months), coinciding with Albania's EU accession negotiation timeline and Chapter 23 obligations. The service contract value is up to EUR 13,000,000. The assignment is classified under CPV 75231000 (Judicial services), with additional CPVs 75100000 (Administration services), 75110000 (General public services), and 75231100 (Law-courts-related administrative services). An options clause allows up to 50% of the initial contract value to be entrusted to the same contractor for similar services without a new procurement.

## Strategic Notes For FS

- **Continuation contract:** EU4Justice II directly follows EU4Justice I (2023–2026), meaning the incumbent consortium has institutional knowledge — bidding firms should assess whether they were part of EU4Justice I or can credibly challenge it.
- **High quality weighting (80/20):** The quality-dominant award formula rewards depth of technical track record over price — favours firms with demonstrated EU accession / justice sector reform credentials.
- **Chapter 23 / EU accession linkage:** Deliverables are directly tied to Albania's EU accession milestones — high political visibility and low likelihood of programme cancellation.
- **Options clause (50%):** The option to extend up to 50% of the initial contract without new procurement means a strong Phase 1 performance could yield an additional EUR 6.5M without recompetition.
- **IPA III multi-year framework:** IPA III runs to 2027 with further programming expected — firms establishing presence in EU4Justice II are well-positioned for successor programmes in Albania and across the Western Balkans.

## Risks / Concerns

- **Restricted procedure / shortlist of 4–8:** Competition is selective but still significant; firms without a verifiable EU judicial/rule-of-law TA track record are unlikely to pass selection.
- **Incumbent advantage:** As a continuation of EU4Justice I, the previous consortium has inside knowledge of institutions, counterparts, and outstanding reform issues.
- **Full ToR not on TED page:** All deliverables, team profiles, evaluation sub-criteria, and selection criteria are in the procurement documents — must be downloaded from the EU Funding and Tenders Portal before bid preparation.
- **Two-stage procedure:** The request-to-participate (RTP) deadline is 14 August 2026; only shortlisted firms proceed to tender submission — this adds lead time and requires early engagement.
- **English only:** Submissions must be in English; no other language accepted for the participation request.
- **Options clause creates uncertainty:** While positive if awarded, the 50% option also means the contracting authority retains significant flexibility on scope after award.
- **GPA not applicable:** The procurement is not covered by the Government Procurement Agreement — relevant for firms assessing procurement rules applicable to their bid.
