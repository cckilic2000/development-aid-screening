## Executive Summary

The Council of Europe source describes the Joint Project "Strengthening Rights and Conditions of Women in Prisons in Turkiye in line with International and EU Standards." The project is co-funded by the European Union and co-funded and implemented by the Council of Europe, with a stated duration of 45 months and total budget of 4 444 450 EUR. It focuses on gender-sensitive and human rights-based prison management, institutional capacity for penitentiary staff, multi-sectoral cooperation, and rehabilitation and reintegration of women prisoners and probationers.

## Detailed Assignment Summary

The project aims to strengthen protection of and further advance women's rights within Turkiye's penitentiary system by improving prison conditions for women prisoners and ensuring respect for their human rights. It will support implementation of gender-sensitive and human rights-based prison management policies and practices aligned with international standards and EU best practices.

Expected outputs include development and implementation of an integrated model of gender-sensitive and human rights-based prison management; improvement of the institutional framework and capacities of penitentiary staff; improved multi-sectoral cooperation between public institutions, the private sector, and civil society; and strengthened economic and social rehabilitation of women offenders and probationers in pilot prisons and probation directorates.

## Key Deliverables

- Integrated model of gender-sensitive and human rights-based prison management.
- Improved institutional framework and capacities of penitentiary staff on gender-sensitive prison management.
- Improved multi-sectoral cooperation among public institutions, the private sector, and civil society on rehabilitation and reintegration.
- Strengthened economic and social rehabilitation of women offenders and probationers in pilot prisons and probation directorates.

## Capacity Building Components

- Improvement of capacities of penitentiary staff on gender-sensitive prison management.
- Assessment missions reviewing rehabilitation, social reintegration, prison management, and staff training practices.
- Development of gender-sensitive and human rights-based prison management practices.
