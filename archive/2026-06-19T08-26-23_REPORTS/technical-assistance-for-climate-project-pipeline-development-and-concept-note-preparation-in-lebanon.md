## Executive Summary

GGGI Jordan Office is seeking eligible firms for technical assistance on climate project pipeline development and concept note preparation in Lebanon. The assignment supports the "Leveraging the Private Sector for Increased Climate Investment and Strengthened Partnerships in the West Asia Region" project and is aligned with GCF investment criteria and national climate priorities. The tender reference is 100016088 and the submission deadline is 20 July 2026, 16:00 KST.

## Detailed Assignment Summary

The assignment objective is to support development of a pipeline of bankable climate projects by providing technical assistance for project identification, pre-feasibility analysis, and concept note preparation in alignment with GCF investment criteria and national climate priorities. It will support Lebanon's NDA, potential DAE, and relevant stakeholders to strengthen their capacity to develop high-quality climate project concept notes and funding proposals.

The source states that GGGI seeks a competent consulting firm to support development of an initial project pipeline outlining suitable options for a green investment facility, along with establishment and operationalization of a Resource Mobilization Strategy. Two high-quality, submission-ready concept notes aligned with GCF investment criteria and national climate priorities will be developed from the pipeline.

## Key Deliverables

- Initial project pipeline outlining best suitable options for a green investment facility.
- Mechanism for resource mobilization established and operational.
- Resource Mobilization Strategy.
- Two high-quality, submission-ready concept notes aligned with GCF investment criteria and national climate priorities.
- Project identification.
- Pre-feasibility analysis.
- Concept note preparation.

## Capacity Building Components

- Build capacity of private sector direct access applicants and DAEs to achieve accreditation.
- Improve ability to develop projects in alignment with GCF criteria.
- Develop capacity of DAEs, EEs, and other private sector actors to increase engagement in climate finance.
- Improve understanding of financial instruments and innovative deal structures for use of public resources to buy down risk for private sector entry into climate finance.
- Support the NDA, potential DAE, and relevant stakeholders to strengthen capacity to develop high-quality climate project concept notes and funding proposals.
- Knowledge sharing and learning through a regional knowledge management tool.
